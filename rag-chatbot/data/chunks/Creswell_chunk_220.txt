Ethical Issues in the Research Problem Hesse-Biber and Leavy (2006 ) ask, “How do ethical issues enter into your selection of a research problem?” (p. 86). In writing an introduction to a study, the researcher identifies a significant problem or issue to study and presents a rationale for its importance. During the identification of the research problem, it is important to identify a problem that will benefit individuals being studied, one that will be meaningful for others besides the researcher (Punch, 2005). A core idea of action/participatory research is that the inquirer will not further marginalize or disempower the study participants.
