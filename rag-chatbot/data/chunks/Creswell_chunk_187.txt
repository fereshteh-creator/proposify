A unique aspect of her chapter is how she weaves together this paradigm of thinking and the steps in the process of conducting mixed methods research. Thomas, G. (1997). What’s the use of theory? Harvard Educational Review, 67(1), 75-104. Gary Thomas presents a reasoned critique of the use of theory in educational inquiry. He notes the various definitions of theory and maps out four broad uses of theory: (a) as thinking and reflection, (b) as tighter or looser hypotheses, (c) as explanations for adding to knowledge in different fields, and (d) as formally expressed statements in science. Having noted these uses, he then embraces the thesis that theory unnecessarily structures and constrains thought.
