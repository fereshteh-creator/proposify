Thousand Oaks, CA: Sage. --- Seite 58 --- Lawrence Locke, Waneen Spirduso, and Stephen Silverman describe three stages for reviewing the literature: develop the concepts that provide a rationale for the study, develop subtopics for each major concept, and add the most important references that support each concept. They also provide six rules for defining terms in a scholarly study: never invent words, provide definitions early in a proposal, do not use common language forms of words, define words when they are first introduced, and use specific definitions for words.
