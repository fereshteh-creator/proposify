Method Participants The participants were 150 undergraduate women enrolled in both lower-and upper-division courses in sociology, psychology, and communications at a midsized university and a community college, both on the west coast. [The authors described the participants in this study.] Design and Experimental Manipulation This study used a 3 X 2 X 2 factorial design: Orientation of Counselor (nonsexist-humanistic, liberal feminist, or radical feminist) X Statement of Values (implicit or explicit) X Participants’ Identification with Feminism (feminist or nonfeminist). Occasional missing data on particular items were handled by a pairwise deletion procedure.
