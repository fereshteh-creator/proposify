This lowering of the barrel begins with a narrative hook of sufficient generality that the reader understands and can relate to the topic. Beyond this first sentence, it is important to clearly identify the issue(s) or problem(s) that leads to a need for the study. Terenzini et al. (2001) discuss a distinct problem: the struggle to increase the racial and ethnic diversity on U.S. college and university campuses. They note that policies to increase diversity are at “the center of an intense national debate” (p. 509). In applied social science research, problems arise from issues, difficulties, and current practices.
